International Federation of 3D Chess

IF3DC — The Governing Body of Three-Dimensional Chess

Our Mission

The International Federation of 3D Chess (IF3DC) exists to promote, standardize, and advance the game of three-dimensional chess worldwide. Founded to bring structure and competitive integrity to Raumschach and its variants, the IF3DC serves as the governing body for all sanctioned 3D chess competitions.

We believe that extending chess into three dimensions unlocks a new frontier of strategic thinking—one that challenges players to reason spatially, plan across vertical and horizontal planes simultaneously, and master entirely new tactical motifs.

What is 3D Chess?

Three-dimensional chess, or Raumschach (“space chess”), was invented by Dr. Ferdinand Maack in Hamburg, Germany in 1907. Played on a 5×5×5 board comprising five stacked levels, it extends classical chess into the third dimension, introducing new strategic possibilities and the unique Unicorn piece—which moves along triagonal lines through all three axes simultaneously.

Unlike novelty multi-level chess variants, Raumschach is a deeply studied game with over a century of recorded play, published theory, and competitive history.

History

1907

Dr. Ferdinand Maack invents Raumschach in Hamburg, Germany, creating the first rigorous three-dimensional chess variant.

1919

Maack publishes the first formal rules and algebraic notation system for Raumschach, enabling recorded games and correspondence play.

1920s–40s

Raumschach gains a following among chess clubs in Germany and Central Europe, with organized matches and published analysis.

2026

The International Federation of 3D Chess (IF3DC) is established to govern competitive play, standardize rules, and grow the global community.

Board Structure

The Raumschach board consists of five 5×5 levels stacked vertically (levels A through E, bottom to top). Each square is identified by three coordinates: column (a–e), row (1–5), and level (A–E). For example, c3C is column c, row 3, level C (the middle level).

The board contains 125 squares total. Movement through three dimensions creates 26 possible directions from any interior square (compared to 8 in standard chess).

Piece Movements

♔

King

Moves one square in any of the 26 directions: orthogonal, diagonal, and triagonal. Must avoid check.

♕

Queen

Combines the powers of Rook and Bishop. Moves any number of squares along orthogonal or diagonal lines (but not triagonals).

♖

Rook

Moves any number of squares along one axis only—horizontally, forward/back, or vertically between levels.

♗

Bishop

Moves any number of squares along 2-axis diagonals. Stays on its color complex (light or dark squares).

♘

Knight

Leaps in an L-shape: 2 squares along one axis and 1 along another. Can jump over pieces. Works across all three axes.

♞

Unicorn

The piece unique to 3D chess. Moves along triagonal lines—diagonals that pass through all three axes simultaneously. One square or many.

♙

Pawn

Advances one square forward (toward the opponent’s side). Captures one square diagonally forward. Promotes on the opponent’s back rank.

Starting Position

Each player begins with 20 pieces arranged across two levels. White occupies levels A and B; Black occupies levels D and E. Level C (the middle) starts empty.

Each side has: 1 King, 1 Queen, 2 Rooks, 2 Bishops, 2 Knights, 2 Unicorns, and 10 Pawns.

Winning the Game

The objective is the same as classical chess: checkmate the opponent’s King. A player also wins if the opponent resigns. The game is drawn by stalemate, agreement, threefold repetition, or the 50-move rule.

Tournament Format

All IF3DC-sanctioned tournaments use the Swiss system for pairing. Players are matched against opponents of similar rating in each round. Final standings are determined by total points, with tiebreaks resolved by Buchholz score.

Competitive games are played using the standard Raumschach rules as defined in the Official Rules section. Time controls will be announced per event.

Rating System

The IF3DC uses an ELO-based rating system. All rated games played through the official platform contribute to a player’s IF3DC rating. New players start at 1200. View the current standings on the Leaderboard.

About IF3DC Governance
